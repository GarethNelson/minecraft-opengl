Game Description

GAMEPLAY -


I. GOALS:

This game is a tribute to famous Minecraft game written by Markus "Notch" Persson and Jens Bergensten.
The user becomes the main character in the game.
In our version, the main goal of this game is to mine the gold blocks that are hidden in the terrain.
Secondary goals include building your own world and staying alive.
The game is won when ten gold blocks are mined, and lost when you are killed by monsters.


II. GAME MODES:

The game has two modes:
Normal gameplay includes all features - items, monsters, inventory, gold blocks.
World builder mode disables monsters, inventory, and points. The main character cannot die, you have unlimited blocks, all blocks are mined immediately, and the game goes on indefinitely. The object of this mode is to allow users to easily create complex scenes and terrains. These scenes can then be saved, and subsequently loaded as the terrain to normal gameplay, allowing users to essentially create their own levels.


III. MOVEMENT:

The game allows you to move in all directions infinitely. Trees and hills will be generated automatically as you go. In addition, you can jump on terrain that is exactly one block high. Left clicking a selected item (block or creature) will cause you to attack it, right clicking will cause you to place an item (if you have one).
In addition, there are four view options available. More culling will generate frames faster.


IV. BLOCKS:

The game has different blocks (dirt, sand, stone, obsidian, gold, bedrock, and wood). Different blocks have different appearances, as well as different properties. They take different amounts of time to mine (gold takes the longest). Stone and obsidian are not affected by gravity, and so can be used to build structures such as caves. Sand, dirt, and wood, however, are all affected by gravity and will fall if there is no block beneath it. Gold blocks are found in the ground of the terrain, and must be mined and collected to win the game. Bedrock lies at the very bottom of the terrain and cannot be mined.
Blocks are mined by "attacking" them. Mined blocks are automatically added to your inventory.
You can only place as many blocks as you've already mined. Using the bottom control panels allows you to switch between types of blocks.


V. CREATURES:

There are three types of creatures in this game:
Cows move away from you slowly and do not damage your health. "Killing" a cow will allow you to regain health.
Deer move towards you and will damage your health. Killing deer also returns you health.
Bombs also move towards you. If you collide with them, they will do more damage than a deer, but will destroy themselves as well. They do not give you health.

Computer automatically generates terrain (if the user has not made their own in world builder).

Texture Mapping




Texture mapping is used to generate the different types of blocks, terrain, creatures.

On-Screen Control Panel




The bottom pane is an inventory bar. A box will be blank if you have none of that item. The bar displays which block is selected (with a thick white border), and also places that block on the right, as if you were holding it.
Also, the red/white diamonds show you how much health you have.

Procedural and Physically Based Modeling


Our tribute has an infinitely generated terrain - i.e., you can walk infinitely in any direction.
When you walk into terrain that has not been generated yet, it will automatically generate ground, trees, and mountains.

Collision Detection


A ditch out of which the player cannot walk - they must jump.
A reticle selects an enemy to attack.
Enemies are stuck behind a barrier created by the player.
Collision detection is applied to both creatures and the main character. Both creatures/your character are checked for collisions with the blocks in the scene, and cannot walk through blocks. In addition, creatures cause you to lose health when they attack (collide with) you.

Advanced Image-Based Techniques (BillBoarding)




Monsters are created using billboarding.
